Build Your First Physical AI System

Welcome! Ready to bring AI into the real world?

In this activity, you won't just look at AI on a screen. You will give AI a physical body.
You will train an AI model and connect it to a real electronic device. When your AI sees or hears something, your device will react instantly.

You will first train an AI classification model using Google Teachable Machine. Your model can recognize images, body poses, or sounds. Then, you will connect the model's prediction results to a physical computing device, such as an LED, servo motor, or speaker.

How It Works — The 3 Steps

AI systems work just like humans do. Here is how your project will come to life:

👁️

  1. Sense: Your computer's camera or microphone gathers information from the real world.
  2. Think: Your AI model (trained by you using Google Teachable Machine) figures out what it sees or hears.
  3. Act: Your code sends a command to your physical device to make it light up, move, or make a sound!

By the end of this project, you will be a creator who knows how to:

  1. Train your own AI using images, poses, or sounds.
  2. Connect AI predictions to real-world code.
  3. Control physical gadgets using nothing but the power of AI!

How to edit and download your code
  1. Connect your physical computing tool to your computer using a USB cable.
  2. Click "Open MakeCode" below to open the starter project.
  3. Click "Edit Code" (top right) to open the full editor and modify the code.
  4. Click the "Simulator" tab, then click the Download button to send the code to your device.
If the download does not work:
  1. Click the … button next to Download.
  2. Choose Download as a file.
  3. Open This PC on your computer.
  4. Find the downloaded .hex file.
  5. Drag and drop the .hex file into the connected drive, such as MICROBIT (D:).

Step 1: Train your AI Model

  

  


    

      
What is Google Teachable Machine?

      
Teachable Machine is a free tool from Google that lets you train an AI model using your own images, body poses, or sounds — no coding required!

    


    

      

        
How to train and export your model:

        
    
          
  1. Open Google Teachable Machine (button below)
    2. 
          
  2. Choose Image Project, Pose Project, or Audio Project
    3. 
          
  3. Record samples for each class (e.g. Class 1, Class 2, Class 3)
    4. 
          
  4. Click "Train Model" and wait until it finishes
    5. 
          
  5. Click "Export Model" → "Upload my model"
    6. 
          
  6. Click "Copy" to copy your model link
    7. 
          
  7. Come back here and go to Step 3 to paste the link!
    8. 
        

      

      
        Open Google Teachable Machine ↗
      
    


    

      

        
Tips for Training Your AI Model

        
    
          
  1. Collect at least 50 examples for each class. More examples can help your model make better predictions.
    2. 
          
  2. Make each class look or sound clearly different. Show the key feature you want the AI to recognize clearly through the camera or microphone.
    3. 
          
  3. Train your model in the same conditions you will use in your project. Keep the lighting, background, camera angle, microphone distance, and room noise similar.
    4. 
          
  4. Test your model before exporting. Try to get high confidence scores. If the model is not accurate, add more examples and train it again.
    5. 
          
  5. For pose models, make sure your hands, face, body gestures, and movements are clearly visible. Check that the skeleton lines appear correctly. Showing your full body usually works best.
    6.
